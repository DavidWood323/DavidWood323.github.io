About Me

Dave Wood

Age 61 now retired from mainstream career which started in the oilfield North Sea 1988.

After a posting to the Far East in 1991 later relocated from UK to Kota Kinabalu in Malaysia in 1996.

Retired early from the oilfield and returned to the UK in 2014 when my elder son started university.



A Passion for Music that spanned 15 years…..& then it was gone

Started with my kids, something missing from my life was the ability to play an instrument and in 2002 aged 7
my elder boy was happy to start lessons. The teacher said well guitar not going so well but watch this on
drums and for the next 12 years till we left Malaysia in 2014 he never missed lessons.

Younger brother joined shortly taking up bass guitar lessons with the same teacher.

A couple of years on from kids in early training my house started to fill up with drum kits guitars
and the whole band kit and the music teacher now coming to us for the weekly lessons.

Along the way a local professional musician became a close family friend, younger brother on bass
became a weekly regular at open mike playing with most of the artists, older bro joined with a snare
and hi-hat for the open mikes and somehow the family social life was transformed.

Lessons from one local musician and an active social life with another, at home, at our friends and
all about the city of Kota Kinabalu every week became a full on music event for all of us. It became
a norm after a couple of years we all just had an active social life and the music became incidental
yet the foundation of it all. It was and became an extravaganza, the private music lessons were now
full on jam sessions and around town both boys were playing with professionals & performing
on stage and we just kept meeting more and more and more musicians.

I lived in Malaysia but I worked in Thailand. 4 mainstream live genres of Thai music and I love them
all and don’t have time to expand. Thai music was an influence on the home front with the drummer,
and later he developed a passion for Japanese music. Both boys got tours of Thailand and Thai bands in a
life a tourist would rarely see.



Oh dear…it pretty much ended in 2014 with a return to the UK.

Elder boy off to university the younger clogged down with studies and no longer in the music scene.
Younger boy now just finished university I mentioned you gotta pick up that bass and get back to where you were.



Otherwise

Decided to make a return to the oilfield Nov 2022, nicely got my old job back in Thailand & then promptly failed
the offshore medical in Bangkok. A stress test ECG produced an equivocal result and no more offshore job.

Returned to Kota Kinabalu for several weeks after an 8 years absence while waiting for a heart scan in Kuala Lumpur.
Jan 2023, heart scan all good but enough to hang up my rig boots if I can't pass the stress ECG.
